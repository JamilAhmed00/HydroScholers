HydroScholars Projects

Project-1

Home Made Water Filter

This project involves designing and building a simple, effective water filter using easily accessible materials. The goal is to create a cost-effective solution for purifying water at home.

By constructing this filter, participants will gain hands-on experience in water purification methods and understand the principles of filtration, including adsorption, sedimentation, and biological filtration.

Project-2

Rainwater Harvesting System Design

This project involves designing and implementing a rainwater harvesting system to teach high school students about sustainable water management. Students will explore the process of collecting and storing rainwater for future use, learning about the components such as catchment areas, gutters, storage tanks, and filtration systems

Project-3

Local Water Audit

In this project, high school students will conduct a local water audit to assess water usage and identify opportunities for conservation within their community or school. Students will learn how to measure water consumption, analyze patterns of use, and identify areas where water is wasted. The project encourages critical thinking as students develop strategies to reduce water waste, improve efficiency, and promote sustainable practices.

Project-4

Water Quality Testing and Report

In this project, high school students will engage in testing the quality of local water sources to assess their safety and cleanliness. Students will collect water samples from various locations, conduct tests for pH, turbidity, dissolved oxygen, and contaminants such as nitrates and bacteria.
